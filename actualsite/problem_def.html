Whats wrong with the pipes?

Pipe fissures and cracks in natural gas pipelines present significant challenges. Even minor cracks can lead to dangerous gas leaks, posing environmental and safety risks. These leaks are notoriously difficult to locate due to the underground nature of pipelines, often requiring extensive inspections to pinpoint the exact location. This process can take considerable time and involves high costs, especially when traffic or infrastructure needs to be rerouted for safe access. The delays not only increase the risk of further leaks but also disrupt public services and lead to expensive logistical adjustments. With increasing drastic weather events, our natural gas distribution networks are more vulnerable than ever. Natural gas alone makes up nearly 1/3rd of all energy usage in the US, and maintaining these systems is more vital for our society
