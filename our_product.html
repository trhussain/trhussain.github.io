Pipeline Inspection Gauge (PIG) Robot

To address the challenges of inspecting underground pipelines, we are developing a Pipeline Inspection Gauge (PIG) robot. Our PIG robot operates on 4 wheels and uses two BLDC motors for reliable and efficient movement through pipelines. The robot is designed to navigate through pipes, detect fissures and cracks, and provide real-time data back to the operator.

A tethered snake-robot outfitted with an IMU, an ESP32-CAM, an ultrasonic sensors and lighting LEDs that provide feedback to a GUI for users to visually identify pipe cracks. This is intended to work on long distance tunnels so having it tethered enables that long-distance communication

Technical Description

Electrical

The electrical system is designed to power and control the PIG robot, including its sensors and motors. This board drives our robot, giving it the capabilites to do what we want. It also enables a much more compact form factor to minimize the overall robot size It features USB-C, a on-off power switch, 3.3V and 5V power regulation, a LSM6DS0X IMU, a header pin array for a BNO6DS0 IMU breakout board, header pin connectors for driving 2 led strips and 2 ultrasonic sensors, and is all controlled by a ESP32-S3. In addition as backup, we have output power pins that run 7.6V to power a LM298N motor driver in case something fails either electrically or mechanically for the BLDC motors
